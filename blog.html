JAVAFX SHARES GAME

13/03/2018 // ADAM LEHODEY

JavaFX. Once hated, now forever loved. I just can’t get enough of it! It all started on those infamous snow days, where I sat down and thought about the way I hadn’t practiced Java in almost a month! One thing I was very actively doing however was updating this blog, and so I decided to create a blog management system, as I’ve already mentioned. I couldn't just create it with a text based GUI as I normally did, since I would be the one using it and wanted it to be easy and faster than updating the source code manually. So, I started coding in JavaFX, watching various tutorials online and reading a book on the topic. As soon as I had finished, I know I had fallen in love with JavaFX.

This week, I have worked tirelessly to create my first real game and definitely my largest project. The aim of the game is to trade the shares and make a profit - quite simple really. The game gets progressively harder as the markets become more volatile, and you need to grow the starting balance of £1,000,000 as much as possible. One of the creative things I did was use HashMaps to map the line charts for each company onto the name, and then onto the one chart, rather than assigning all of them separately, allowing for greater expandability and flexibility when coding. I also was able to play around a lot with the layout, and created what I believe to be an intuitive and nice looking layout for the game, with information on share price, balance and more. If you’re looking to start coding in JavaFX, I recommend courses on YouTube or books from WHSmith.

One of the final challenges I faced was when I wanted to show my friends the project, only to realise they all needed to have the Java Runtime Environment installed for the Java ByteCode to be understood. Now I’m not sure about you, but if you asked 99% of people what the JRE is, they’d have no idea. So, I managed to package the whole application into one easily runnable .EXE file, in which I bundled the JRE for the application to run off natively, to distribute in one easy and installable package.

RESPONSIVE VIDEO DESIGN

28/02/2018 // ADAM LEHODEY

One of the main features you’ll notice about my website is the fluid, responsive design which works on all device sizes and keeps content flowing. When uploading videos to my blog, it was clear this was a problem as the native youtube embed gives you videos as a static size, so it overflows if ever you’re viewing on a mobile design. Here is the fix:

  1. Get the YouTube video you’d like to embed and copy the embed link, pasting it into where you’d like the content to go.
  2. Create a separate div surrounding this, perhaps calling it ‘Content Box’ or ‘ContentWrapper’ - this div will serve to add padding to the bottom of the iframe to keep the aspect ratio the same.
  3. Create an iframe selector for your iframe, with position absolute.
  4. One issue I encountered was having a large amount of white space which was the padding underneath the video when size wasn’t set to 100%. Therefore, to fix this, create another div and set the max width to your height of video, but use the equivalent value in vw as the width varies less on devices. Then, clip the overflow.
  5. You’re done!
HTML
<div class="V-Blog"><div> class="Wrapper"><iframe> width="560" height="315" src="https://youtu.be/ayuooyWPEUc?t=30s" frameborder="0" allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen></iframe></div></div>
CSS
.Wrapper {position: relative; padding-bottom: 75%; padding-top: 25px; height: 0; overflow: clip;} .videoWrapper iframe { position: absolute; top: 0; left: 2.5%; width: 95%; height: 69%;}.V-Blog {height: 41vw; overflow: hidden;}

Enjoy and feel free to ask any questions!

FINANCE SPRING SCHOOL

20/02/2018 // ADAM LEHODEY

Over the past week, while many people spent their February half term on the other side of the world, on the beach and with their sunglasses down, I decided to stay in London, where the average temperature was 3°C. Not that it bothered me, since I was at one of the world’s greatest Universities, University College London, partaking in a Business and Finance spring school, which covered so many aspects of the Financial World and the things pathways you can take.

On the first day, we were greeted by UCL’s student ambassadors, which were extremely welcoming and friendly, and you really could talk to them as people. I made the most of the opportunity to talk to as many of them as possible and ask endless questions on University study and general questions on studying in London, all of which is proving to be incredibly helpful.

In regards to the finance side of things, we were put into groups, working on a 4 day project where we had to explore a particular area of the financial sector, the roles involves, the degree paths which you can take, and more. One thing which I found very intriguing was the number of degrees which allowed you to enter jobs in the Finance and The City, including things like Computer Science, Linguistics and Politics. This is something to keep in mind when choosing your degree, showing that what really matters in the industry are your people skills and your willingness to learn. I look forward to attending lots more University taster sessions, but meanwhile, this term I’m focusing 100% on my maths skills to achieve the A* and gain a place at an incredible university like UCL.
